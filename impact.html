What we track

Attendance registers, workshop topics, referrals, follow-ups, distribution logs, stakeholder participation, and beneficiary feedback.

Safeguarding and ethics

We prioritize dignity, privacy, and child protection. All evidence is collected responsibly and shared with consent where applicable.

How we measure impact

A donor-friendly structure that connects resources to real outcomes.

Inputs

Funding, volunteers, materials, partnerships, and trained facilitators.

Activities

Workshops, awareness campaigns, counselling support, outreach and referrals.

Outputs

Sessions delivered, beneficiaries reached, resources distributed, support provided.

Outcomes

Improved awareness, safer schools, stronger families, better access to support.

Learning

We use feedback and data to improve programme quality and strengthen delivery.

Reporting

Partner-ready summaries: activities, reach, evidence, photos, and results updates.

Where we create change

Focused impact areas that reflect community priorities and real needs.

Safe Schools

Anti-bullying education, reporting pathways, and learner protection awareness.

Health Awareness

HIV/AIDS education, stigma reduction, and health support information access.

Family Support

Strengthening caregivers with knowledge, referrals and community support networks.

Child Protection

Safety-first approaches that prioritize dignity and wellbeing of children.

Youth Empowerment

Skills, mentorship, leadership development and confidence-building activities.

Partnerships

Working with schools, community leaders, and institutions to scale impact responsibly.
